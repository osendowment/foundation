The Open Source Endowment (OSE) aims to solve the [open source sustainability crisis](https://openpath.quest/2024/the-open-source-sustainability-crisis/) by
providing maintainers of critical open source software with a source of funding that is sustainable, systemic and scalable. 

- **🌱 Sustainable**: Ensures long-term, predictable cash flow, independent of economic and political volatility
- **🔗 Systemic**: Prioritizes critical yet underfunded OSS, rather than large/popular ones or specific supply chains
- **🚀 Scalable**: Operates as an efficient next-gen charity designed for scale, not a like a typical nonprofit

OSE is the world's first endowment exclusively dedicated to open source. It is co-founded by an inclusive community of tech founders, executives, investors, and software engineers who care about OSS.﻿﻿ For now, we continue to accept [co-founding individual donors](https://github.com/osendowment/foundation/issues/1). 

## How it works

|Step|Description|
|-|-|
|Fundraising|The Open Source Endowment Foundation raises money from individual and institutional donors, including companies and foundations. In the initial stage, we acquire only individuals as co-founding donors.|
|Investments|All donations are not spent but invested in a low-risk portfolio consisting of stocks, bonds, and other securities. It is planned to be done via licensed US-based asset managers. These assets provide a yearly return (target ~ 5-7% APY), which can be used for grants.|
|Selection|Based on the open grant-making model, [co-developed with donors](https://github.com/osendowment/foundation/issues/2), we identify underfunded critical open source projects and select target grantees. Part of the money the Foundation makes from its assets (target ~ 5%) every year is distributed as grants.|
|Monitoring|We regularly measure the impact of our grants on selected open source projects and the global software supply chain's sustainability. This data is used to continuously refine our selection model in an open source way – collectively fix its bugs and increase efficiency.|

## Governance

### Members

Individual donors who contribute at least $1000 to the endowment fund qualify as the OSE members. Members advise the OSE board of on strategic matters, such as the grant selection model, and in the future will also nominate several board members. OSE currently has over 20 co-founding donors with commitments qualifying for the membership.
