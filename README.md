The [Open Source Endowment](https://endowment.dev/) aims to solve the [open source sustainability crisis](https://openpath.quest/2024/the-open-source-sustainability-crisis/) by
providing maintainers of critical open source software with a source of funding that is sustainable, systemic and scalable. 

- **🌱 Sustainable**: Ensures long-term, predictable cash flow, independent of economic and political volatility
- **🔗 Systemic**: Prioritizes critical yet underfunded OSS, rather than large/popular ones or specific supply chains
- **🚀 Scalable**: Operates as an efficient next-gen charity designed for scale, not a like a typical non-profit foundation

As the world's first-ever endowment exclusively dedicated to open source, it is co-founded and powered by an inclusive community of tech founders, executives, investors, and software engineers who care about OSS sustainability.﻿﻿

## How it works

|Stage|Description|
|-|-|
|Fundraising|The Open Source Endowment Foundation raises money from individual and institutional donors, including companies and foundations. In the initial stage, we acquire only individuals as co-founding donors.|
|Investments|All incoming donations are not spent but invested in a low-risk portfolio consisting of stocks, bonds, and other securities. It is planned to be done via licensed US-based asset managers. These assets provide a yearly return (target ~ 5-7% APY), which can be used only for nonprofit causes.|
|Selection|Based on the open grant-making model, co-developed with donors, we identify underfunded critical open source projects and select grantees among their maintainers. Part of the money the Foundation makes from its assets (target ~ 5%/year) every year is distributed to grantees.|
|Monitoring|We regularly measure the impact of our grants on selected open source projects and the global software supply chain's sustainability. This data is used to improve our model, fix its bugs, and increase efficiency.|

## Governance

### Members

Individual donors who contributed at least US$1000 to the endowment fund become the OSE members. They have a direct ability to participate in decisions taken by the OSE. The aim of this threshold is to ensure that decisions are made by individuals that have personally committed to the OSE's goals and have skin in the game. It also helps to co-design the OSE together with passionate individuals, who are ready to materially support their OSS views.

## FAQ

**Why did nobody had built an open source software endowment?**
1. The endowment model is requires a 20+ year planning horizon, because usually only ~5% of donations are spent every year. Most existing charities focus on shorter-term results and can't afford such an approach.
2. Endowments fit well only for open-ended goals such as the sustainability of open source software generally. Meanwhile, a lot of existing OSS funding is attached with to areas with limited lifespans like operating systems, language ecosystems, or specific developers.
3. Building a lasting grassroots OSS endowment requires a rare intersection of multiple areas of expertise: software development, open source, fundraising, endowments, developer-focused startup scaling, charity, and so on. Most philanthropists are not familiar with open source values and their challenges — this problem did not exist 25 years ago.
